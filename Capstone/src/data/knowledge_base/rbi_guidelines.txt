RBI & REGULATORY GUIDELINES (summarised for FIAA knowledge base)
================================================================
Note: This is an educational summary for a capstone project, not legal advice.

RBI MASTER DIRECTION — FRAUD RISK MANAGEMENT
Banks must have a board-approved fraud risk management policy. Frauds must be
classified, reported to the RBI through the prescribed returns, and reviewed by
the board's special committee. Early-warning signals must trigger investigation.

REPORTING TIMELINES
- Frauds of Rs 1 lakh and above: report to RBI within the prescribed window after
  detection through the central fraud reporting system.
- Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) are filed with FIU-IND under the PMLA when
  a transaction appears linked to proceeds of crime, regardless of amount.
- Cash Transaction Reports (CTRs) cover aggregated cash above the threshold.

CUSTOMER LIABILITY (Limited Liability Circular)
- Zero liability for the customer where the loss is due to bank negligence or a
  third-party breach reported within 3 working days.
- Limited liability applies on a sliding scale for delayed reporting.
- The burden of proving customer liability rests with the bank.

DIGITAL PAYMENT SECURITY CONTROLS
Banks must implement multi-factor authentication, transaction velocity controls,
device binding, and real-time fraud monitoring. Adaptive authentication should
step up for anomalous behaviour such as new devices or unusual geolocation.

CROSS-BORDER & SWIFT
For unauthorised cross-border wires, time is critical: the SWIFT recall request
should be initiated immediately (commonly within a ~2 hour operational window)
through the correspondent banking channel. Beneficiary-side freezes depend on the
receiving jurisdiction's cooperation, so speed materially affects recovery.

KYC / AML
Customer Due Diligence (CDD) must be risk-based. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
applies to high-risk customers and to transactions involving high-risk corridors.
Periodic re-KYC is mandatory; mismatched or thin KYC is an onboarding red flag.

HIGH-RISK CORRIDORS
Transfers to jurisdictions flagged by FATF or with weak AML enforcement warrant
enhanced scrutiny. A high-value transfer to such a corridor, combined with
behavioural anomalies, is a strong indicator of layering.

GRIEVANCE & TURNAROUND
Disputed electronic transactions must be resolved within the regulator-specified
turnaround time; failure attracts compensation obligations to the customer.
