FRAUD RESPONSE PLAYBOOKS — Fraud Intelligence AI Assistant
==========================================================

PLAYBOOK P-01 — Card-Not-Present (CNP) Fraud
Trigger signals: multiple declined CVV attempts, foreign merchant, small test
transaction followed by large purchase. Procedure: block the card immediately,
reverse the disputed authorisation, raise a chargeback, notify the cardholder
through a verified channel, and file an internal fraud ticket. Regulatory: report
to RBI under the master direction on customer protection if customer is liable
for zero amount when reported within 3 working days.

PLAYBOOK P-02 — Account Takeover (ATO)
Trigger signals: new device login, password + beneficiary change within minutes,
disabled SMS alerts, login from anomalous geolocation. Procedure: freeze digital
channels, force credential reset over a verified channel, reverse beneficiary
changes, review last 30 days of transactions, and escalate to the fraud desk.
ATO combined with an immediate high-value transfer is always HIGH risk.

PLAYBOOK P-03 — Mule Account Detection
Trigger signals: dormant account suddenly receiving and forwarding funds, many
small credits aggregated then wired out, beneficiary is a recently opened account.
Procedure: place a debit freeze, flag the beneficiary network, file a Suspicious
Transaction Report (STR) with FIU-IND, and preserve the transaction graph for
investigators.

PLAYBOOK P-04 — Phishing / Vishing Compromise
Trigger signals: customer reports sharing OTP, transaction shortly after an
inbound call, beneficiary added during the suspected call window. Procedure:
reverse where possible, educate the customer, reset credentials, and capture the
fraudster's collection account for blacklisting.

PLAYBOOK P-05 — UPI Collect / QR Fraud
Trigger signals: collect requests disguised as payments, QR scan that debits
instead of credits. Procedure: dispute through NPCI, block the VPA, and warn the
customer about collect-request semantics.

PLAYBOOK P-06 — Velocity / Structuring
Trigger signals: many transactions just under a reporting threshold within a short
window (smurfing). Procedure: aggregate across accounts, evaluate for structuring,
and file an STR if the pattern indicates deliberate threshold avoidance.

PLAYBOOK P-07 — Cross-Border Wire / Layering
Trigger signals: large transfer to a high-risk corridor, velocity breach, geo
anomaly, new device. Procedure: freeze the beneficiary account, initiate a SWIFT
recall WITHIN THE 2-HOUR WINDOW, file an STR with FIU-IND, and notify the
correspondent bank with full case details. This is the highest-urgency playbook;
the SWIFT recall window is unforgiving and lost time is lost money.

PLAYBOOK P-08 — Synthetic Identity
Trigger signals: thin-file applicant, mismatched KYC documents, address shared by
many accounts. Procedure: re-verify KYC, hold disbursement, and refer to the
onboarding risk team.

ESCALATION MATRIX
Risk score >= 7 routes to human review with a recommended action set. Risk score
below 7 may auto-close with a logged rationale. Any HIGH-weight regulatory
deadline (e.g. SWIFT recall) forces human review regardless of score.

CONFIDENCE GUIDANCE
Confidence is HIGH when multiple independent high-weight signals corroborate and a
close precedent exists. Confidence is LOW when the knowledge base returns no
relevant precedent — in that case degrade gracefully and recommend manual review.
